# Purpose

This document defines the overall gameplay design of FaulkerVerse.

It serves as the high-level blueprint for how the game's major systems work together to create a living, open-world life simulation.

This document intentionally avoids low-level implementation details. Those belong in the dedicated system documents (Ride System, Economy, NPCs, Businesses, etc.).

---

# Game Overview

FaulkerVerse is an open-world life simulation RPG set in an authentic recreation of downtown St. Louis.

Players begin with very little and gradually build a life through work, entrepreneurship, exploration, relationships, and smart decision making.

There is no predetermined path to success.

The game presents opportunities.

The player chooses which opportunities to pursue.

Every system exists to reinforce the illusion that the player is living inside a functioning city rather than progressing through scripted content.

---

# Core Gameplay Loop

The core gameplay loop of FaulkerVerse is built around opportunity rather than missions.

```
Explore

↓

Discover Opportunities

↓

Accept Work

↓

Earn Money

↓

Improve Skills & Equipment

↓

Unlock New Opportunities

↓

Purchase Assets

↓

Expand Businesses

↓

Increase Freedom

↓

Repeat
```

The player's goals evolve naturally over time.

Early gameplay focuses on survival and learning the city.

Mid-game focuses on financial growth.

Late-game focuses on ownership, management, and expanding influence.

---

# Gameplay Pillars

Every gameplay system should reinforce one or more of these pillars.

## Exploration

The city rewards curiosity.

Players should constantly discover new locations, shortcuts, businesses, events, and opportunities.

Exploration should always feel worthwhile.

---

## Opportunity

The world continuously generates opportunities.

Transportation requests.

Temporary jobs.

Business investments.

Special events.

Unexpected encounters.

Players are never forced into one activity.

---

## Entrepreneurship

Money is a tool.

Players gradually move from working for money to making money work for them.

The long-term goal is expanding freedom rather than accumulating wealth alone.

---

## Simulation

The city exists independently of the player.

NPCs follow schedules.

Businesses operate normally.

Traffic changes throughout the day.

Weather influences behavior.

The player participates in the simulation rather than controlling it.

---

## Emergent Stories

The most memorable moments should not be scripted.

Independent systems interact to create experiences unique to every player.

Players should tell stories about what happened—not about what mission they completed.

---

# Player Journey

The player's experience naturally evolves through several phases.

## Phase 1 — Arrival

Learn the city.

Explore.

Earn the first dollars.

Acquire basic transportation.

Become familiar with downtown.

---

## Phase 2 — Growth

Improve equipment.

Purchase better vehicles.

Build reputation.

Unlock additional opportunities.

Begin specializing.

---

## Phase 3 — Ownership

Purchase businesses.

Hire employees.

Manage multiple income sources.

Expand operations.

Build long-term wealth.

---

## Phase 4 — Legacy

Operate a network of businesses.

Shape portions of the local economy.

Continue exploring.

Discover new opportunities.

Experiment with different careers.

The game never truly ends.

# Careers

FaulkerVerse supports multiple career paths.

Examples include:

- Transportation
- Delivery
- Hospitality
- Retail
- Food Service
- Maintenance
- Entertainment
- Tourism
- Business Ownership

Additional careers should be introduced over time.

Each career should connect naturally to the economy and other gameplay systems.

---

# Transportation

Transportation serves as one of the earliest careers available.

It introduces players to:

- Navigation
- City layout
- Reputation
- Customer interactions
- Time management
- Weather
- Traffic
- The economy

Transportation remains valuable throughout the game by expanding into fleet ownership and business management.

---

# Economy

The economy drives player progression.

Money allows players to:

- Purchase vehicles
- Buy equipment
- Acquire businesses
- Hire employees
- Unlock additional gameplay opportunities

Money should always feel valuable.

The goal is not to become rich.

The goal is to increase freedom.

---

# Businesses

Businesses represent long-term progression.

Players should gradually evolve from:

Employee

↓

Independent Worker

↓

Small Business Owner

↓

Employer

↓

Multi-Business Owner

↓

Investor

Every business should introduce new gameplay rather than simply generating passive income.

Ownership should create additional decisions and responsibilities.

---

# Progression

Progression is based on expanding possibilities rather than increasing statistics.

Progression includes:

- Knowledge
- Reputation
- Financial stability
- Equipment
- Vehicles
- Businesses
- Employees
- Influence

Players should feel more capable over time without becoming disconnected from the simulation.

---

# Failure

Failure should create setbacks instead of ending the game.

Examples include:

- Financial loss
- Vehicle damage
- Missed opportunities
- Poor reputation
- Reduced customer demand
- Business expenses

Players should always have a path toward recovery.

Failure should encourage learning rather than punishment.

---

# Player Choice

There is rarely one correct decision.

The game should encourage experimentation.

Different players should naturally build different lives.

Every meaningful choice should influence future opportunities.

---

# Long-Term Gameplay

The objective is not to finish FaulkerVerse.

The objective is to continue building a life inside the city.

Players should always have another goal waiting:

- Another vehicle.
- Another employee.
- Another business.
- Another neighborhood.
- Another opportunity.
- Another career.

The simulation should continue expanding alongside the player.

---

# Future Expansion

The architecture should support future additions without requiring major redesign.

Examples include:

- Multiplayer
- Additional cities
- Public transportation
- Construction
- Property ownership
- Seasonal events
- Dynamic economy
- Advanced NPC relationships
- Community-created content
- Mod support

Every expansion should strengthen existing systems rather than replace them.

---

# Design Principles

Every gameplay system should satisfy at least one of the following:

- Encourage exploration.
- Present meaningful choices.
- Connect with existing systems.
- Create new opportunities.
- Reward long-term thinking.
- Increase player freedom.
- Generate emergent stories.

Features that do not support these principles should be reconsidered.

---

# Definition of Success

A successful gameplay session is one where the player becomes immersed in the life of the city rather than thinking about game mechanics.

The player should finish each session with new experiences, new stories, and new goals.

The game succeeds when players say:

"I was only planning to drive a few passengers..."

"...and somehow I spent three hours building my business, exploring downtown, discovering a new shortcut, and watching the city come alive."

That feeling is the essence of FaulkerVerse.
