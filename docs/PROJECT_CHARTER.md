# FaulkerVerse Project Charter

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Active  
**Last Updated:** 2026-07-19

This charter defines the vision, philosophy, and guiding principles for the development of FaulkerVerse. Every design, technical, and gameplay decision should align with the principles established in this document.

---

# Mission

Build an open-world life simulation RPG where players create their own story through work, entrepreneurship, exploration, and relationships inside an authentic recreation of downtown St. Louis.

FaulkerVerse is not built around a single scripted storyline.

Instead, it is built around interconnected systems that allow players to create their own path.

---

# Core Promise

Every time a player launches FaulkerVerse, they should feel like they are stepping into a city that was already alive before they arrived and will continue living after they leave.

The player is not the center of the world.

The player becomes part of it.

---

# Vision

The long-term goal of FaulkerVerse is to create the most authentic and believable digital recreation of downtown St. Louis ever built for an open-world game.

Players should recognize the streets, landmarks, neighborhoods, and businesses. Someone familiar with downtown should be able to navigate much of the city from memory.

The city is designed to function as a living ecosystem rather than a backdrop. Businesses open and close, people travel to work, sporting events generate crowds, weather influences behavior, and traffic patterns evolve throughout the day.

The player steps into this world as one participant among many—not as the force that makes it exist.

---

# Player Agency

Players should rarely be told what they must do.

Instead, the game should present opportunities.

The player decides which opportunities to pursue.

The world should encourage curiosity rather than force progression.

---

# Design Pillars

Every major feature should reinforce one or more of these principles.

## Freedom

Players choose how they earn money, spend their time, and build their future.

There is no single correct way to play FaulkerVerse.

---

## Living World

NPCs have schedules.

Businesses have purpose.

Transportation has demand.

Events affect the city.

The simulation continues regardless of the player's actions.

The player should be able to observe the world changing even when they choose not to participate.

---

## Authenticity

The world should faithfully represent downtown St. Louis wherever practical.

Road layouts, intersections, landmarks, parks, and building footprints should reflect reality while remaining enjoyable to explore.

Gameplay always takes priority while preserving the authenticity of the world.

---

## Meaningful Progression

Every dollar earned should matter.

Every vehicle upgrade should matter.

Every business purchased should unlock new opportunities.

The player's success should feel earned.

Progression should unlock possibilities rather than simply increase numbers.

---

## Systems Over Scripts

FaulkerVerse is a systems-driven game.

Instead of relying on scripted missions to create memorable moments, independent systems interact with one another to produce unique experiences.

Examples include:

- Weather influencing ride demand
- Sporting events increasing traffic
- Businesses creating employment
- NPC schedules affecting transportation
- The economy responding to player actions

The most memorable stories should emerge naturally from these interactions.

The goal is not to tell the player a story.

The goal is to build a world where stories naturally emerge.

---

# Technical Philosophy

The project is developed one stable milestone at a time.

Every sprint should leave the game in a playable state.

Working systems should be extended rather than rewritten whenever possible.

Documentation precedes implementation.

Architecture should prioritize maintainability over shortcuts.

Systems should be modular, reusable, and data-driven whenever practical.

---

# Authenticity Policy

Authenticity does not require perfect realism.

The objective is to recreate the feeling of downtown St. Louis rather than simulate every detail.

Whenever realism conflicts with enjoyable gameplay, gameplay takes priority while preserving the spirit of the city.

---

# Success Criteria

FaulkerVerse succeeds when players stop thinking about game mechanics and begin thinking about life inside the city.

A successful player might spend an evening:

- Driving golf cart passengers after a Cardinals game.
- Expanding their transportation company.
- Purchasing another business.
- Exploring downtown.
- Simply enjoying the atmosphere.

The player should feel as though they are living inside a believable city rather than completing a checklist of missions.

---

# Guiding Questions

Before implementing any new feature, ask:

- Does this make the world feel more alive?
- Does this create meaningful choices for the player?
- Does it integrate with existing systems instead of standing alone?
- Can this feature grow over time without requiring a redesign?

If the answer is "yes" to most of these questions, the feature probably belongs in FaulkerVerse.

---

# What FaulkerVerse Is Not

FaulkerVerse is not a golf cart simulator.

It is not a taxi simulator.

It is not a city builder.

It is not a business tycoon game.

It is not a driving game.

It is not a traditional RPG.

Instead, FaulkerVerse combines elements from all of those genres into a living world where transportation, business ownership, exploration, and simulation work together to create unique player stories.

---

# Long-Term Vision

FaulkerVerse is designed to grow for many years.

New careers...

New businesses...

New districts...

New transportation methods...

New simulation systems...

...should all integrate naturally into the existing world rather than replace it.

The project should evolve by expanding the simulation, not by abandoning previous systems.

---

# The FaulkerVerse Test

Before implementing any feature, ask:

- Does this make the world feel more alive?
- Does this create meaningful choices?
- Does this connect to existing systems?
- Will players create stories because of this feature?

If the answer is "yes" to most of these questions, the feature probably belongs in FaulkerVerse.

---

# Final Principle

Every decision made during the development of FaulkerVerse should strengthen the illusion that the player is living inside a believable, evolving city.

Technology exists to serve that vision.

Every system should contribute to that goal.
