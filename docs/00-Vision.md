# FaulkerVerse Vision

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Active  
**Last Updated:** 2026-07-19

---

# Purpose

This document describes the intended player experience of FaulkerVerse.

Unlike the Project Charter, which defines the philosophy and guiding principles of development, this document focuses on what it should feel like to play the game.

Every gameplay system, feature, mechanic, and technical decision should support this vision.

---

# The Dream

FaulkerVerse is an open-world life simulation RPG set within an authentic recreation of downtown St. Louis.

Players are not cast as heroes destined to save the city.

They are ordinary people arriving in a living city filled with opportunity.

The world already exists.

Businesses are operating.

People are working.

Traffic is moving.

Events are taking place.

Life continues whether the player participates or not.

The player's story begins the moment they decide how they want to live inside that world.

---

# The Player Experience

The first hour should create curiosity.

The first week should create ambition.

The hundredth hour should create attachment.

Players should constantly discover new places, new opportunities, new careers, and new ways to grow.

Success should never feel predetermined.

Instead, players should build their own story through exploration, hard work, smart decisions, and a little luck.

---

# A Living City

Downtown St. Louis is more than a map.

It is the primary character of FaulkerVerse.

The city breathes.

Morning commuters create traffic.

Restaurants become busy during lunch.

Office buildings empty at the end of the workday.

Sporting events flood downtown with visitors.

Concerts change transportation demand.

Weather alters how people move throughout the city.

Nightlife transforms familiar streets into entirely different experiences.

The city should feel alive even when the player chooses to simply observe.

---

# Authenticity

Authenticity is one of the defining goals of FaulkerVerse.

Players familiar with downtown St. Louis should recognize its streets, landmarks, parks, neighborhoods, and major buildings.

The objective is not to create a perfect architectural replica.

The objective is to recreate the feeling of being there.

Whenever gameplay and realism conflict, gameplay should be improved while preserving the identity of the city.

---

# Player Freedom

There is no single correct way to play FaulkerVerse.

Players should always have meaningful choices.

One player may begin by transporting passengers in a golf cart.

Another may pursue business ownership.

Another may spend hours simply exploring the city.

Another may focus on collecting vehicles or building wealth.

None of these paths are considered the "main" game.

The player's choices create their own progression.

---

# Progression

Progression should feel natural.

Players earn money.

Money creates opportunities.

Opportunities unlock new careers, businesses, vehicles, equipment, and experiences.

Success should expand possibilities rather than restrict them.

The game should reward creativity, persistence, and thoughtful decision making.

---

# Systems Create Stories

FaulkerVerse is designed around simulation rather than scripted storytelling.

Independent systems interact continuously.

Weather...

Traffic...

Business activity...

Sporting events...

NPC schedules...

The economy...

Together these systems create situations that no developer specifically scripted.

The goal is not to tell every player the same story.

The goal is to create a world where every player develops different stories worth sharing.

---

# Long-Term Growth

FaulkerVerse is intended to evolve for many years.

The initial release represents only the foundation.

Future updates should expand the simulation rather than replace it.

Possible expansions include:

- Additional careers
- New transportation systems
- More businesses
- Expanded neighborhoods
- Additional districts
- Multiplayer experiences
- Community-created content
- Seasonal events
- Dynamic city evolution

Every new feature should integrate naturally with the systems that already exist.

---

# Design Philosophy

Players should rarely feel as though they are completing objectives.

Instead, they should feel as though they are living inside a believable city.

The best moments in FaulkerVerse should emerge naturally.

Helping strangers after a baseball game.

Discovering a shortcut through downtown.

Watching a thunderstorm roll across the skyline.

Growing a small transportation business into a thriving company.

Exploring a neighborhood they have never visited before.

These moments should not feel scripted.

They should feel earned.

---

# Measuring Success

A successful FaulkerVerse session is not measured by quests completed.

It is measured by experiences created.

If players finish a session thinking:

"I'll just drive a few passengers."

…and two hours later realize they ended up exploring a new neighborhood, attending an event, buying a new business, and watching the sunset over downtown…

Then FaulkerVerse has achieved its goal.

---

# Vision Statement

FaulkerVerse exists to create a believable digital city where players build their own lives through exploration, opportunity, entrepreneurship, and interconnected simulation systems.

The city is the stage.

The systems create the stories.

The player chooses the path.
