# FaulkerVerse Ride System

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Active  
**Last Updated:** 2026-07-19

---

# Purpose

The Ride System is the player's primary introduction to the world of FaulkerVerse.

It serves as both an early-game career and a long-term business opportunity.

While transporting passengers is the core activity, the Ride System is ultimately designed to teach the player how the city functions. Through driving, the player naturally learns the streets, landmarks, traffic patterns, weather effects, business locations, and event schedules.

The Ride System should remain engaging from the player's first ride to the ownership of an entire transportation company.

---

# Design Philosophy

The Ride System is not a taxi simulator.

It is a transportation career within a living city.

Driving passengers should feel like participating in the daily life of downtown St. Louis rather than completing repetitive missions.

Every ride should be slightly different because the city itself is constantly changing.

---

# Player Journey

The Ride System evolves through several stages.

## Stage 1 — Independent Driver

The player begins by accepting individual ride requests.

Goals include:

- Learning the city
- Building reputation
- Earning initial income
- Purchasing better equipment
- Improving driving efficiency

---

## Stage 2 — Professional Driver

The player gains experience and develops a reputation.

Benefits may include:

- Better-paying customers
- Priority ride requests
- Larger events
- Business contracts
- Hotel partnerships

---

## Stage 3 — Fleet Owner

The player purchases additional golf carts or vehicles.

Responsibilities include:

- Hiring drivers
- Scheduling workers
- Maintaining vehicles
- Managing operating costs
- Expanding service areas

The player transitions from earning money through labor to earning money through management.

---

## Stage 4 — Transportation Company

The player owns an established transportation business.

Gameplay shifts toward:

- Fleet expansion
- Employee management
- Marketing
- Strategic growth
- Long-term planning

Driving remains an option but is no longer required.

---

# Ride Requests

Ride requests should originate naturally from the simulation.

Examples include:

- Hotels
- Stadiums
- Restaurants
- Office buildings
- Parking garages
- Tourist attractions
- Entertainment venues
- Residential areas
- Special events

Requests should reflect what is happening within the city rather than appearing randomly.

---

# Dispatch System

Ride requests are generated by demand.

Factors influencing demand include:

- Time of day
- Weather
- Sporting events
- Concerts
- Holidays
- Traffic
- Nearby businesses
- Tourism
- Player reputation

Demand should fluctuate naturally throughout the day.

---

# Passenger Types

Not every passenger is the same.

Examples include:

- Tourists
- Office workers
- Hotel guests
- Families
- Business travelers
- Event attendees
- Restaurant patrons
- VIP customers

Different passenger types may have different expectations, destinations, and tipping behavior.

---

# Reputation

Every completed ride contributes to the player's reputation.

Positive factors include:

- Safe driving
- Fast service
- Clean vehicles
- Customer satisfaction
- Reliability

Negative factors include:

- Dangerous driving
- Late arrivals
- Vehicle neglect
- Cancelled rides
- Customer complaints

A stronger reputation unlocks additional opportunities.

---

# Pricing

Ride prices should be influenced by multiple factors.

Examples include:

- Distance
- Time
- Event demand
- Weather
- Passenger type
- Traffic conditions

Busy periods naturally increase earning potential.

---

# Tips

Passengers may choose to leave gratuities.

Factors include:

- Driving quality
- Arrival time
- Vehicle cleanliness
- Passenger satisfaction
- Weather
- Special events

Tips should feel earned rather than random.

---

# Navigation

The Ride System teaches the player the city.

Initially, players may rely heavily on GPS.

Over time, experienced players should naturally learn:

- Faster routes
- Event traffic
- Parking locations
- Hotel entrances
- Stadium exits
- Hidden shortcuts

Knowledge of the city becomes a valuable gameplay skill.

---

# Events

Large events significantly affect transportation demand.

Examples include:

- Cardinals games
- Blues games
- Concerts
- Festivals
- Conventions
- Holiday celebrations

These events should create:

- Heavy pedestrian traffic
- Increased ride requests
- Road closures
- Parking shortages
- Higher earning opportunities

Events should feel exciting rather than scripted.

---

# Weather

Weather directly influences transportation.

Examples:

Sunny weather:

- More walking
- Lower transportation demand

Rain:

- Increased ride requests
- Slower traffic
- Higher tips

Snow (future):

- Difficult driving
- Reduced visibility
- Emergency demand

Weather should change both strategy and atmosphere.

---

# Vehicles

The Ride System supports multiple transportation types.

Initial release:

- Golf carts

Future expansion:

- Shuttle vans
- Small buses
- Luxury transportation
- Water transportation
- Specialty event vehicles

Each vehicle type should offer different advantages and trade-offs.

---

# Fleet Management

Business ownership introduces a second layer of gameplay.

Players manage:

- Drivers
- Scheduling
- Vehicle maintenance
- Repairs
- Fuel or charging
- Insurance
- Revenue
- Expansion

Management should create meaningful decisions rather than passive income.

---

# Integration With Other Systems

The Ride System interacts with nearly every major system in FaulkerVerse.

Including:

- Economy
- Weather
- NPC schedules
- Businesses
- Events
- Traffic
- Reputation
- Time of day
- Player progression

The Ride System should never feel isolated.

It is one part of a larger living simulation.

---

# Long-Term Vision

Transportation should remain enjoyable throughout the entire game.

Even wealthy players should have reasons to continue driving occasionally.

Examples include:

- Helping during major events
- Testing new vehicles
- Covering busy shifts
- Exploring newly added districts
- Completing personal goals

Driving should always remain fun, even after it is no longer financially necessary.

---

# Definition of Success

The Ride System succeeds when players stop thinking about completing rides and start thinking like transportation professionals.

They anticipate demand before it happens.

They recognize event schedules.

They know the fastest routes.

They remember regular customers.

They understand the rhythm of the city.

When players begin making decisions based on experience instead of user interface prompts, the Ride System has achieved its purpose.

---

# Ride System Vision Statement

Transportation is more than a way to earn money.

It is the player's first relationship with the city.

Every passenger introduces a new destination.

Every destination teaches another street.

Every street becomes another memory.

The Ride System transforms the city from a map into a place the player truly knows.
